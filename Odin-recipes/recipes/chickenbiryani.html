Chicken Biryani Recipe

This chicken biryani recipe combines aromatic spices, tender chicken, and fragrant basmati rice for a delicious one-pot meal. Ingredients For the Chicken Marinate:

  • 1 kg chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 teaspoons red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 2 teaspoons garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Juice of 1 lemon
For the Rice:
  • 3 cups basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2-3 whole cloves
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 star anise (optional)
  • Salt to taste
For Cooking:
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk (optional)
  • Fried onions for garnish (optional)
  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, mint, and lemon juice. Add the chicken pieces and coat them well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, preferably overnight for better flavor.
  2. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for about 30 minutes, then drain.
  3. Cook the Onions: In a heavy-bottomed pot, heat ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and fry until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove half for garnishing later.
  4. Cook the Chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the pot with the remaining onions. Cook on medium heat until the chicken is browned and cooked through, about 10-15 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  5. Layer the Biryani: In a separate pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the whole spices (cloves, cardamom, bay leaf, star anise) and salt. Add the soaked and drained rice to the boiling water and cook until it is about 70% done (the rice should still have a bite to it). Drain the rice.
  6. Layer the partially cooked rice over the chicken in the pot. Drizzle saffron milk over the top and sprinkle the reserved fried onions. Cover the pot tightly with a lid.
  7. Dum Cooking: Reduce the heat to low and cook for about 20-25 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking. You can place a heavy pan on top of the lid to trap the steam.
  8. Serve: Once done, gently fluff the biryani with a fork. Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) and salad.
Enjoy your homemade chicken biryani, a delightful dish that brings the flavors of Indian cuisine to your table!
